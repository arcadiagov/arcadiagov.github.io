Arcadian Citizenship Ordinance

As ratified under the Authority of the Lord High King

Chapter I: Definition and Scope

Article 1. Citizenship in the Kingdom Realm of Arcadia is a formal and binding legal status granted solely by the Crown and administered through the Office of Citizenship Affairs.

Article 2. No individual shall be considered a subject of the Realm without express documentation bearing the seal of the Crown and signature of the Chief Secretary of Citizenship Affairs.

Article 3. All subjects of Arcadia owe fealty to His Lordship the Lord High King and are entitled to the rights, duties, and protections enumerated under the Arcadian Constitution and Royal Decrees.

Chapter II: Categories of Citizenship

Article 4. Citizenship may be granted under one of the following categories:

  • (a) Birthright Citizenship
  • (b) Citizenship by Descent
  • (c) Citizenship by Naturalization
  • (d) Honorary Citizenship (by royal decree only)

Article 5. Dual citizenship shall be permitted only where no conflict of loyalty is present. The Office of Citizenship Affairs may revoke or suspend citizenship where such conflict arises.

Chapter III: Eligibility and Application

Article 6. All applicants for naturalization must:

  • Submit a formal petition through the Office of Citizenship Affairs
  • Swear allegiance to the Crown and Constitution
  • Pass a cultural and historical examination administered by the Crown
  • Have no affiliations with hostile states or subversive factions

Article 7. The Office of Citizenship Affairs reserves the right to investigate, delay, or deny any application for any reason deemed necessary for the security or dignity of the Realm.

Article 8. The Lord High King may directly grant or deny citizenship at any time without justification, superseding all other provisions.

Chapter IV: Rights and Duties of Citizens

Article 9. Citizens of Arcadia shall enjoy the following rights:

  • Legal protection under Arcadian law
  • Participation in national consultation forums
  • Right to petition the Crown directly
  • Access to governmental programs and honors

Article 10. Citizens must uphold the following duties:

  • Maintain loyalty to the Crown and Realm
  • Report threats to national sovereignty
  • Refrain from hostile activities against the Kingdom
  • Comply with all royal decrees and legal statutes

Chapter V: Revocation and Rejection

Article 11. Citizenship may be revoked by the Chief Secretary of Citizenship Affairs if:

  • The individual engages in treason or espionage
  • The individual holds dual allegiance with a hostile power
  • The citizenship was granted based on false information

Article 12. Rejected applications are final and non-appealable, except through executive intervention by the Lord High King.

Article 13. Denied applicants may not reapply for a minimum period of 3 years unless granted permission by royal dispensation.

Chapter VI: Administration and Oversight

Article 14. The Office of Citizenship Affairs shall be headed by a Chief Secretary appointed by the Lord High King.

Article 15. The Office shall maintain a secure registry of all citizens and ensure the integrity of the application and revocation processes.

Article 16. The Chief Secretary shall report directly to the Crown and may issue provisional directives in urgent matters of national allegiance.

Ratification Clause

This Ordinance is enacted by royal assent and carries the full force of law throughout the Kingdom Realm of Arcadia, effective immediately upon publication.

— By Order of His Lordship, the Lord High King of Arcadia
— A. M. Delora, Chief Secretary of Citizenship Affairs
