Case T-027-RAMP – Empire vs. Steeves

⚖️ Confidential: Prosecution File – Lennox Steeves

Countering the Defense

  • Voluntary confession includes criminal self-incrimination, undermining any future plea of innocence.
  • Claiming terrorist identity establishes intent to subvert national peace and lawful order.
  • Dismissive tone of confession supports prosecution's case on recklessness and lack of remorse.

Prosecution Strategy Points

  • Use video/audio/archived confession: “I totally lied. I'm a terrorist. MWAHAHAHAHAHA….”
  • Introduce historical pattern of similar violent rhetoric from social and message records.
  • Link confession to acts listed in arrest warrant for context and legal substantiation.
  • Portray defendant as destabilizing threat with intent to cause fear and unrest.

Prepared Speeches (Max 60s)

Opening Statement

The accused has declared themselves a terrorist in a recorded confession, mocking justice and endangering national security. Such behavior demands a firm, lawful response.

Rebuttal to Defense Minimization

This is not a joke. In Arcadia, we do not dismiss violent self-incrimination. We prosecute it.

Closing Remarks

Let the tribunal remember that confessions of terrorism are not satire—they are warning signs. Prosecute accordingly and preserve the peace.

Evidence Highlights

  • Confession: “I totally lied. I'm a terrorist. MWAHAHAHAHA…” (Video Record ID: MLR-001)
  • Relevant arrest warrant lists pattern of subversive and destabilizing acts.
  • Archived posts matching tone and rhetoric used in confession.

Countering the Defense

  • Intent does not negate unauthorized system breach—good intentions are irrelevant under RAMP Cyber Protocol 9.4.
  • System “upgrades” made post-incident do not absolve prior criminal acts. This is mitigation, not exoneration.
  • No ethical hacker registry exists; unauthorized intrusion cannot be post-labeled as ethical without pre-clearance.
  • Defense fails to provide independent verification of Steeves’s stated purpose or intent.

Prosecution Strategy Points

  • Leverage digital forensics to show repeated and deliberate unauthorized access patterns.
  • Use testimony from internal IT officers showing no request, contact, or attempted clearance by Steeves.
  • Frame the act as part of a larger pattern of techno-vigilantism which undermines legal process.
  • Undercut the narrative of "ethical hacking" by citing international norms and Arcadian case law.

Prepared Speeches (Max 60s)

Opening Statement (58s)

Members of the tribunal, this is not a case of curiosity gone too far. It is about accountability in a digital age. Mr. Steeves did not ask permission, he did not report his findings, and he bypassed layers of classified infrastructure with full knowledge of its protected status. This is not vigilance—it’s vigilantism.

Rebuttal to "Good Intent" (47s)

Good intentions are not a defense in Arcadian law. If they were, any citizen could justify breaching nuclear codes to “test” them. We do not criminalize intent alone, but we absolutely prosecute actions. This was a deliberate, repeated incursion into a protected zone.

Closing Remarks (59s)

You have heard the defense speak of hope, but justice is not about hope—it is about law. Mr. Steeves’s actions broke that law. No title, no goal, no claimed ethic can excuse a breach of the sovereign security network. Find him accountable, not because he is evil—but because the law was broken, and we are a nation of laws.

Evidence Highlights

  • Log records showing three breaches within 36 hours, escalating in clearance level.
  • Direct access to restricted files unrelated to the vulnerabilities he later reported.
  • Steeves’s internal messages referencing "outsmarting the firewall" (archived, Evidence ID: C-2275).
  • No reporting to RAMP following breach until after investigation began.

Countering the Defense

  • Direct written threat invalidates any claim of nonviolent intent.
  • Email language clearly demonstrates premeditated menace and implied intent to do harm.
  • Pattern of criminal behavior listed in the arrest warrant further discredits any claim of misinterpretation.

Prosecution Strategy Points

  • Leverage explicit threat: "I'm a kill you I know where you live :)" as evidence of criminal intent and targeted harassment.
  • Show pattern of prior offenses in arrest warrant—establish escalation and repeat behavior.
  • Highlight psychological harm and personal endangerment to the victim as aggravating factors.
  • Present evidence of digital stalking and known address tracking as part of pre-assault behavior.
